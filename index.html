Common mental health disorders

Below is a list of the most common mental health disorders people suffer from.
Each card describes the various symptoms of the listed disorder.

Anxiety

Common anxiety symptoms are:

1)Excessive worrying, panic
2)Restlessness, fatigue
3)Difficulty concentrating, irritability
3)Tense muscles
4)Trouble falling or staying asleep
5)Panic attacks
6)Avoiding social situations
7)Nausea, dizziness, shortness of breath

HelpLine
Depression

Common depression symptoms are:

1)Continuous sadness/hoplessness
2)Irritability and intolerance
3)No motivation, interest or energy
4)Not getting any enjoyment out of life
5)Neglecting hobbies and social activities
6)Changes in appetite, weight and sleep
7)Avoiding contact with friends and family
8)Suicidal thoughts/harming yourself

HelpLine
Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder

Common ADHD symptoms are:

1)Short attention span
2)Physical, verbal, or emotional hyperactivity and fidgting
3)Disorganization and forgetfulness
4)Impulsiveness
5)Trouble multitasking, executive dysfunction
6)Inability to control anger/frustration
7)Trouble completing tasks/frequent procrastination
8)Distractibility

HelpLine
Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder

Common PTSD symptoms are:

1)Re-experiencing(flashbacks, nightmares, distressing images, sensations)
2)Avoidance of talking/thinking about places and activities connected to the trauma
3)Guilt, shame
4)Irritability, anger outbursts
5)Other mental health problems (i.e. phobias)
6)Being easily startled/frightened, being on guard
7)Self harming/destructive behavior

HelpLine
Bipolar Disorder

Common BD symptoms are:

1)Periods of depression
2)Manic episodes
3)Feeling happy/elated/overjoyed
4)Being full of energy
5)Not sleeping/eating
6)Being delusional, having hallucinations and disturbed/illogical thinking
7)Doing/saying things out of character
8)Doing things with disastrous consequences

HelpLine
Obessive Compulsive Disorder

Common OCD symptoms are:

1)Fear of contamination/dirt
2)Difficulty tolerating uncertainty
3)Needing things orderly/symmetrical
4)Aggressive/horrific thoughts
5)Doubts about having done certain things
6)Compulsive washing & cleaning
7)Compulsive checking and counting
8)Following strict routine
9)Demanding reassurance

Mental health and COVID-19


"Fear, worry, and stress are normal responses to perceived or real threats, and at times when we are faced with uncertainty or the unknown. So it is normal and understandable that people are experiencing fear in the context of the COVID-19 pandemic.
Faced with new realities of working from home, temporary unemployment, home-schooling of children, and lack of physical contact with other family members, friends and colleagues, it is important that we look after our mental, as well as our physical, health."
-WHO
